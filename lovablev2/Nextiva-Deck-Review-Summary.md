# Nextiva Deck Review with Hari — Summary & Action Items

**Meeting:** March 17, 2026 | **Duration:** 2h 0m 54s  
**Participants:** Hari Ravichandran, Tomas Gorny, Anne Brennan, Drew Schulz, Seksom Suriyapa, Yaniv Masjedi

**Purpose:** Deck review for debt refinancing (targeting lenders like Servus to take out Goldman’s ~$250M facility)

---

## Executive Summary

Hari’s main message: **last year the fight was the numbers; this year it’s AI defensibility.** Lenders are nervous about “SaaSpocalypse”—AI replacing SaaS. Nextiva’s edge: **infrastructure + data + regulated industries** are hard to replicate with LLMs or vibe coding.

---

## Slide-by-Slide Feedback

### At a Glance / Opening Slides
| Slide | Feedback | Action |
|-------|----------|--------|
| MRR / Churn (1.6%) | **Don’t lead with this.** Weakest point for credit. Show **NDR (92%)** instead. Lenders benchmark against high-NDR companies. | Remove subscriber/churn metrics from front. Use net dollar retention as headline. |
| NDR Presentation | Emphasize dollar retention, not gross churn. Year 1 churn is high; by year 2–3 it stabilizes (0.6%). | Show NDR 91% → 94% with voiceover. Lead with that story; leave cohorts for diligence. |

### AI Defensibility (Critical)
| Topic | Feedback | Action |
|-------|----------|--------|
| AI Tailwind vs Headwind | Market is paranoid. Lenders worry AI will replace SaaS. You must show **why AI is a tailwind, not a headwind.** | Add slides on: (1) infrastructure/telephony, (2) regulated industries, (3) proprietary data. |
| Third-Party Validation | Use Gartner or similar: “companies that adapt to AI win vs die.” Put Nextiva in the top-right (defensible). | Find and add Gartner/report chart. |
| Infrastructure Argument | **“Nobody can touch me because I’ve got all this data and infrastructure.”** Telephony/infra is hard for LLMs to replace. | Emphasize: telephony, physical infra, regulated. |
| Regulated Industries | **Add: “Industry is regulated—vibe coding kids cannot put software in.”** Strong defense point. | Add “regulated” to defensibility slide (infra + data + regulated). |
| Data Moat | Data is always “fresh.” Unlike static CRM, Nextiva sits at the edge of conversation. | Strengthen “conversational data mode” and “always fresh data” copy. |
| Per-Line Pricing Risk | Lenders worry per-employee-per-month = downside if workforce shrinks. | Frame outcome/usage pricing as growing % of revenue (e.g., 30% by FY28). |

### Defensible Moat Slide
- **Keep:** Conversational data mode, platform economics (land & expand), no concentration.
- **Add:** Infrastructure; shift “platform economics” to “outcome-based pricing”; regulated.
- **Change:** Replace 1.6% with NDR. Add outcome-based revenue % (e.g., 20–30%).

### EBITDA & Financial Slides
| Topic | Feedback | Action |
|-------|----------|--------|
| EBITDA Projection | **Flatten after ~$100M.** Don’t show big step-ups; lenders will tie covenants to those numbers. | Show 94 → 99 → 103 type path, not 94 → 185. Cut off at FY27. |
| FCF / Severance | Add severance back, show FCF ~35, footnote one-time costs. Lenders want to see EBITDA→cash conversion. | Show unlevered FCF; footnote interest/severance. |
| LQA (Last Quarter Annualized) | Use pro forma for April actions (headcount) pulled back to Jan–Mar to boost TTM. | Pro forma March with April actions; annualize Jan–Apr. |
| Cutoff | Don’t show FY28–30 to lenders. Use LQA bridge. | Cut financials at FY27; remove long-term targets for debt deck. |
| First Call | Show only: historical, TTM, LQA, and path to 80–90 EBITDA in next 12 months. | Two slides max for first meeting. |
